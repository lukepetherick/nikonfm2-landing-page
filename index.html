The Nikon FM2

The best 35mm SLR of all time?

The Nikon FM2 has been a workhorse of the analogue SLR world since its introduction in 1982

Features

  • Extremely durable copper-aluminium-silicon body
  • Precision-tapered high strength shutter blades and self lubricating mirror/shutter mechanism
  • Close tolerance assembly and minimal lubrication allowing operation in temperatures from -40℃ to 50℃
  • Shutter speed of 1-1/4000th of a second with 1/250th X-sync
  • In-built light meter showing 0.5 to 1 stop of over/under exposure
  • Complete Nikon F mount lens support
  • Full mechanical functionality with manual exposure - batteries are only used to power the light meter

"It’s a fantastic camera. It’s certainly one of the best film SLRs that Nikon ever made, and it easily sits among the greatest 35mm film SLRs made by anyone, ever. That’s not hyperbole or gushing or overselling. The FM2 is just really good"

-- casualphotophile.com
